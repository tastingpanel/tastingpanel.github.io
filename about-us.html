Who we are

We are happy to know you are happy

The Tasting Panel is a sister company to Vibrand Research, and part of the Vibrand Group of research companies.

In our work at Vibrand, we have been running tasting panels for our clients for a while, and realised that there was a need to broaden this service.

Simply put, The Tasting Panel offers tasting panels! We convene (typically) 200 consumers in a relaxed, home environment and offer them blind tastings of products. They are asked to fill out a questionnaire, which is carefully designed by our expert team. The complexity of the questionnaire depends on the nature of the project (see our toolbox), but we prefer to keep things as simple as possible.

We place great value on evaluating products in the environment which they are consumed. We don’t believe in testing booths, laboratory settings, external sensory elimination and the like. We are interested in evaluating food and drink in a real life environment. We believe this delivers better, more realistic results and allows for a more relaxed environment. It also brings cost savings that are passed onto our clients. So no white coats are allowed at our tastings!

And while we call ourselves the Tasting Panel, we do also offer product testing – from razors to cosmetics; from washing powder to floor cleaner we will find a way to test it.

So whether you are evaluating whether to launch a new product, tweak an existing one, launch a limited edition, evaluate competitor products against you own – The Tasting Panel has the answer.

Worried there is too much garlic in your Chicken a la King? That your cheese is too rubbery? That your yoghurt is too lumpy?

That your kitchen cleaner leaves residue; that your face cream is too thick

Want to know whether your competitor's ice cream is better than yours? Should yours be creamier? What variants are likely to hit the spot this summer? You have three limited editions to consider and you can only launch one?

You’re a retailer and you want to be sure that your own brands are better than your competitor own brands?

Want to know whether your beer if too fizzy, your whiskey too oaky, your wine too thin, your RTD too sweet?

We’ll recommend the right approach from our toolbox (see here) - from the simple to the complex; from the 2 product face off to multi product preference mapping; from JAR (Just About Right) to cluster to principal component analysis, we have the tool to fit the need.

To answer these and a thousand other questions you may have, The Tasting Panel has the answer.
